Engineering for Humanity: Helping Elders Age in Place through Partnerships for Healthy Living

This integrated course at Olin College is co-taught by an anthropologist and a designer, and stresses the importance of using anthropological methods to design the right product for a user. It partners a team of students with community volunteers aged 65 or older. Throughout the semester, the students learn about aging and the difficulties encountered by elders both through a literature review and by interacting with the community volunteers. At the culmination of the semester, the students (in conjunction with the volunteers) design and build solutions to reduce or eliminate some of the difficulties directly impacting the older volunteers.

For a more in depth description please visit: http://e4h.olin.edu and http://e4h.olin.edu/spring2013.html
