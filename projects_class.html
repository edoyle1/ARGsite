Engineering for Humanity: Helping Elders Age in Place through Partnerships for Healthy Living

This course at Olin College is co-taught by an anthropologist and a designer, and stresses the importance of using anthropological methods to design the right product for a user. It is designed to partner students with community volunteers aged 65 or older. Throughout the semester, the students learn about aging and the difficulties encountered by elders both by reading academic papers and interacting with the community volunteers. At the culmination of the semester, the students (in conjunction with the volunteers) design and build solutions to reduce or eliminate some of the difficulties that the older volunteers are facing.

For a more in depth description please visit: http://e4h.olin.edu
